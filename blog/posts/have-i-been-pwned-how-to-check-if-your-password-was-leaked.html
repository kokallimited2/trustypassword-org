Have I Been Pwned? How to Check if Your Password Was Leaked

Data breaches happen every day. Find out if your email and passwords have been compromised — and what to do about it.

Advertisement

Your email and passwords might already be for sale on the dark web without you knowing. Here's how to find out and what to do.

What Is a Data Breach?

When a website or service is hacked, attackers often steal the database of usernames and passwords. These lists get sold, traded, and eventually made public. Billions of credentials are circulating online right now.

How to Check If You've Been Breached

The easiest way is to visit haveibeenpwned.com — a free service run by security researcher Troy Hunt. Enter your email address and it tells you which breaches it appeared in.

You can also use our built-in breach checker above, which uses the same HIBP API.

What To Do If You've Been Pwned

  1. Change the password immediately on the breached site
  2. Change it everywhere else you used the same password
  3. Enable two-factor authentication on all important accounts
  4. Use a password manager to ensure every account has a unique password

Prevention

The best protection is never reusing passwords. Use a generator to create unique random passwords for every site, and a manager to store them safely.
