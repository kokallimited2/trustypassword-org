Why Strong Passwords Are Your First Line of Defence

Most data breaches happen because of weak or reused passwords. Here's how to protect yourself today.

Advertisement

In 2024 alone, over 1.5 billion passwords were exposed in data breaches. The single most effective thing you can do to protect yourself is to use a strong, unique password for every account.

What Makes a Password Strong?

A strong password has three qualities: length (at least 16 characters), complexity (uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols), and uniqueness (never reused across sites).

The Maths Behind Password Strength

An 8-character password with only lowercase letters has 208 billion possible combinations. An 8-character password using all character types has 722 trillion. A 16-character mixed password has more combinations than atoms in the observable universe.

Common Password Mistakes

  • Using dictionary words or names
  • Adding numbers at the end (Password1 is not secure)
  • Reusing passwords across multiple sites
  • Using personal information like birthdays or pet names

The Solution

Use a password generator to create truly random passwords, and a password manager to remember them. You only need to remember one master password.
