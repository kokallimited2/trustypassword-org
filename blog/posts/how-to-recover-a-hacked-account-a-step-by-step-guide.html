How to Recover a Hacked Account: A Step-by-Step Guide

Discovering you've been hacked is stressful. Here's exactly what to do in the first 24 hours to limit the damage.

Advertisement

You've just discovered an account has been compromised. Don't panic — here's your action plan.

The First 30 Minutes

1. Regain Access

Use the "Forgot Password" function immediately. Check the email on file — if you still have access, reset the password now. If your email is also compromised, use the backup recovery options (phone number, backup codes).

2. Change the Password

Generate a new strong, unique password — 16+ random characters. Do not reuse any previous password or variation of it.

3. Revoke All Sessions

Most platforms have a "sign out all devices" or "active sessions" page. Use it. This immediately disconnects the attacker even if they still have your old password.

The Next 24 Hours

4. Enable 2FA

If it wasn't on, enable it now. An authenticator app is best.

5. Check for Damage

Look for unauthorised orders, transfers, messages sent in your name, or changes to account details (recovery email, phone number).

6. Change Passwords on Linked Accounts

If you used the same password anywhere else, change those immediately. Check all accounts that use this email as a login.

Prevention Going Forward

Use a password manager with unique passwords for every site, enable 2FA everywhere, and check haveibeenpwned.com monthly.
